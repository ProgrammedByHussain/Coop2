Work Term Report

Introduction

Welcome to my co-op experience website! I am now in my third year of Computer Science and on this website, I share the highlights of my second work term. I stayed at the same placement as my previous term, but I was given the opportunity to collaborate with another individual at the Co-op named Andrew. The main theme of this semester was perseverance and collaboration.

Employer

I was employed again at the registrar's office on the 3rd floor of the university centre. I worked in the scheduling department under my supervisor Tammy, and as previously mentioned, there was another student with me this time. I was grateful to be at the University again as it is a place that feels like a home away from home. One thing I need to mention is that I was given this co-op placement originally in the summer as part of the president's Co-op initiative. Thus, I appreciate my employer allowing me to have this opportunity in the first place. I was brought back under an official co-op placement for the fall, but I wouldn’t have even been given this opportunity to come back if it weren’t for the initial initiative.

Goals

My goals for this work term focused on enhancing my teamwork, quantitative literacy, critical thinking, and written communication skills. I aimed to collaborate effectively with my co-op partner, Andrew, quantify the impact of my work to demonstrate its value, thoroughly understand tasks by asking questions, and produce clear and comprehensive documentation. I create these goals to improve my job performance and prepare me for future opportunities.

I developed specific goals relating to my job tasks, such as dividing responsibilities with my co-op partner based on our strengths, allowing one of us to take the lead when we see fit. Additionally, I set goals to quantify my work outcomes and track the measurable effects of my contributions. I also wanted to improve my communication skills through documentation and ensure a deep understanding of tasks by actively clarifying expectations.

Reflecting on my goals, I improved my teamwork and written communication skills and gained a better understanding of quantifying my work's impact. Myself and Andrew were in contact a lot of the time, as we were working remotely for several days, and this emphasis on improving communication allowed us to do well regardless of not being in the same place. We made sure to ask questions when we needed help, including the team at Infosilem. However, I could further improve my ability to fully quantify my contributions, as this requires more practice and experience. Overall, these outcomes have created a strong foundation for my future growth.

Job

During my previous co-op placement, I worked as a Scheduling Systems Support alongside my co-op partner, Andrew, under the supervision of Tammy. My responsibilities varied widely, including both physical tasks, such as heavy lifting in the office, and more technical duties, such as database merging.

One significant project involved merging the fall and winter databases. This project required us to explore and understand various API documentation for Infosilem Timetabler, as we initially lacked the knowledge to complete the task. Through extensive reading and learning, we successfully developed a solution to merge the databases. We did this by using SQL queries after several tries with other solutions. We were attempting to access the APIs but given the time constraints, we had to get creative and try other ways. All in all, I’m incredibly glad for the skills and experience we developed in the journey of trying to merge these two databases.

Additionally, I worked extensively with Python scripts to assist Tammy with various tasks. These included getting rid of duplicate entries and verifying the accuracy of registration numbers to ensure data integrity.

Conclusion

My co-op placement as a Scheduling Systems Support provided a valuable opportunity to develop both technical and professional skills. Collaborating with my co-op partner, Andrew, and assisting Tammy with various tasks also taught me the importance of teamwork and communication in a workplace setting. The valuable lessons I learned from the trial and error with the merging of the databases was priceless as myself and Andrew learned to stay composed, even when things weren’t working well and we managed to get the bulk of the project done in a relatively quick turnaround.

Acknowledgments

I would like to mention a massive thank you to both Tammy and Andrew throughout this recent Co-op term. This semester was different from the previous in the sense that I was now always working with someone else, and I found out how much I enjoy working and collaborating with others who are passionate about the same things I am.
